```
- Ensure sufficient contrast (WCAG AA minimum: 4.5:1)
- Don't rely on bold alone—use positioning and structure
- Consider larger font sizes (16px minimum)
- Avoid all-bold paragraphs (reduces contrast)

### For Cognitive Accessibility

- Keep bold points concise (under 10 words ideal)
- Limit to 3-5 points (avoid cognitive overload)
- Use consistent bolding patterns
- Provide alternative formats if requested

### Plain Text Fallback

For recipients requiring plain text:
- Use `*asterisks*` for emphasis
- Use UPPERCASE sparingly (can seem aggressive)
- Use structural spacing and numbering
- Lead with key points

### WCAG Compliance

While email doesn't have formal WCAG requirements, consider:
- **Perceivable:** Information can be accessed through multiple senses
- **Operable:** Interface can be used by various input methods
- **Understandable:** Content and interface are clear
- **Robust:** Works across technologies

**Recommendation:** Always test your emails with a screen reader if possible.
```

```markdown
## Cultural & Contextual Considerations

### International Communication

**Be aware that formatting preferences vary by culture:**

- **Western (US/UK/EU):** Generally accepts bold for emphasis
- **East Asian:** May view excessive formatting as aggressive
- **Middle Eastern:** Consider context and relationship hierarchy
- **Latin American:** Relationship-building often precedes directness

**Recommendations:**
- When in doubt, err on the side of simplicity
- Mirror the recipient's formatting style
- Build relationship before using assertive formatting
- Consider cultural context of recipient

### Industry Variations

- **Academia:** Often prefers plain text or minimal formatting
- **Legal:** Has specific formatting standards (often prescribed)
- **Finance:** Prefers structured, conservative formatting
- **Creative Industries:** More flexibility with formatting
- **Government/Military:** Often requires plain text for security
- **Healthcare:** HIPAA compliance may limit HTML

### Relationship Context

**First Contact:**
- Use minimal formatting
- Focus on clarity over emphasis
- Build credibility before being bold

**Established Relationship:**
- Can use more formatting
- Understand recipient's preferences
- Adapt based on past interactions

**Power Dynamics:**
- Junior to senior: More conservative
- Peer to peer: Standard approach
- Senior to junior: Be mindful of tone

### Language Considerations

- **RTL Languages (Arabic, Hebrew):** Formatting may render differently
- **Character Sets:** Some characters don't support bold (CJK in some clients)
- **Translation:** Formatting may be lost in machine translation
```
