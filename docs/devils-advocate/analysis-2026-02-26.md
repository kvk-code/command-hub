## 5. ⚠️ EDGE CASES NOT PROPERLY HANDLED

The `professional-email-formatting.md` guide provides comprehensive base guidance but doesn't address:

#### Edge Case: Very Short Emails
- **Issue:** Email with < 50 words
- **Problem:** Guide recommends 3-5 bold points, but short emails may have < 3 important points

- **Solution Needed:** Clarify that quality > quantity, fewer bold points acceptable for short messages

#### Edge Case: Multiple Stakeholder Audiences
- **Issue:** Email to diverse recipients (exec, technical, external)
- **Problem:** Different stakeholders need different emphasis
- **Not Addressed:** How to choose what to bold when audiences vary
- **Solution Needed:** Guidance on prioritizing universal vs. audience-specific points

#### Edge Case: International/Non-Native Recipients
- **Issue:** Recipients with varying English proficiency
- **Problem:** Bolding may not provide same benefit
- **Not Addressed:** Cultural context for emphasis, formality levels
- **Solution Needed:** Section on international communication considerations

#### Edge Case: Plain-Text Email Clients
- **Issue:** Some users/organizations prefer/require plain-text
- **Problem:** HTML bolding won't render
- **Not Addressed:** Plain-text alternatives for emphasis
- **Solution Needed:** Plain-text formatting strategies (CAPS, *asterisks*, etc.)

#### Edge Case: Mobile vs. Desktop Rendering
- **Issue:** Email appearance differs on mobile devices
- **Problem:** Bolding may be less effective on small screens
- **Not Addressed:** Mobile optimization considerations
- **Solution Needed:** Responsive email formatting guidance

#### Edge Case: Accessibility Tools
- **Issue:** Screen readers, text-to-speech, high-contrast modes
- **Problem:** Visual emphasis may not translate
- **Not Addressed:** How to ensure emphasis works with assistive technology
- **Solution Needed:** Semantic HTML guidance (`<strong>` vs `<b>`)

#### Edge Case: Email Client Compatibility
- **Issue:** Different clients (Outlook, Gmail, Apple Mail, etc.) render differently
- **Problem:** HTML may break or display inconsistently
- **Not Addressed:** Testing/compatibility matrix
- **Solution Needed:** List of tested clients or fallback strategies
